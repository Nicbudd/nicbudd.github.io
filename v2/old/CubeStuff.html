y' U R U R' U2 R U R' //pair4
U2 F R U R' U' F' //OLL (45, "T")
U2 M2' U M' U2 M U M2' //PLL (Ua Perm)

So when I started the solve, I found a very easy XCross on white. Normally I don't make a XCrosses in solves, but this one was so obvious and easy to make that it would be stupid to not make it. After inspection, where I did y x2, all I had to do was 3 moves, F2 L' R.

The first pair I saw was oddly the Orange-Blue pair. I stupidly took the edge out by rotating y' and doing L' U' L. This was bad because I took it out unpaired, which meant I had to spend extra time pairing it up, with y U' R U2 R' before inserting it with U2 R U' R'.

While I was inserting the Orange-Blue pair I noticed the Red-Green pair was paired up before I broke it up. Normally I am not good with lookahead, so this was convenient because I now knew where those were. Unfortunately I didn't do the easier Blue-Red pair because I was focused on the Red-Green pair. I did y2, which is bad because I could've done it from the back, but right now I am terrible at doing pairs from the back, and then U2 R U R' to pair them up, and U2 R U' R' to insert it.

The last pair was pretty standard for me. I rotated to face the slot with y', moved the corner out of the way with U and took the edge out with R U R', which paired the pieces up. I then did U2 R U R' to insert it.

OLL was pretty easy, and it was a case I already know very well. This is the point in the video that I sit up and realize that I'm gonna get an amazing time.

PLL was a U-Perm, which is where I kinda messed up. I used to use the alg, but I had just started to retrain myself to do the version. I didn't have very good finger tricks for it at the time, and I was nervous seeing that the time was at under 10 seconds and I just had to do U Perm. Maybe if I had done it faster I would've gotten a sub-12 time, and that would be my overall PB. Whatever the case I still got an amazing time and I'm proud of it.
