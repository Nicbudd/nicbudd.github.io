CubingUSA Nationals 2018

July 27-29, 2018

Recently, I was fortunate enough to travel from my home in New Hampshire all the way to Utah to compete in the CubingUSA Nationals 2018 (some times shortened to "USA Nationals" or just "Nats").

I met plenty of great people. I got to talk to current and former world record holders, such as Feliks Zemdegs, Mats Valk, Kevin Hays, Kian Mansour, Patrick Ponce, Sebastian Weyer, Rami Sbahi, Jayden McNeill, Chris Olson, Drew Brads, and other famous people, such as Dylan Wang (known as J Perm), Jesse Hartnett (known as JRCuber), Andrew Nathenson (known as ColorfulPockets), and so many other notable people. I also gained quite a few great friends there who I still keep in touch with.

When I competed, I was very nervous and didn't break any of my 3x3 Personal Records (abbreviated as PR), or make it past the first round in any event, but I did beat both of my records in 2x2.

Below are the reconstructions of my 3x3 solves. The tables show exactly what moves I made, the time it took me to do each step, the turn speed of each step (TPS, or turns per second), and the amount of turns each step took. I will also give a commentary on each solve. The chart and commentary is aimed towards advanced speedsolvers and will probably be hard to understand if you aren't one.

This starting solve wasn't that great. It was my slowest cross out of all the solves and was a terrible solution. I only fully planned out 2 cross pieces and partially planned out the other 2. I had to pause to figure out where to put those pieces. I paused and freaked out after my cross, on my first pair because of course I wasn't tracking those pieces. The rest of F2L went mostly smoothly, except I did the same terrible solution for Pair 3 and 4. I took the edge out in a way that made it not paired up with the corner, which means I wasted more moves pairing it up again. The last layer was pretty standard, but I would like to be able to recognize PLL from 2 sides and learn the M2 fingertrick.

The second solve I was much happier with. I did an OK cross (I am terrible at cross) and then had incredibly fast TPS for F2L. I managed to have a 9.07 TPS for the last pair, which is by far the highest TPS I have ever achieved during an official 3x3 solve. OLL and PLL went very well, and I ended up getting my fastest time of that average, 15.76, and highest overall TPS ever at 5.58.
