FMC

FMC (officially known as "3x3 Fewest Moves") is an acronym for "Fewest Move Challenge", and is an official World Cubing Association (WCA) event where competitors are given a 3x3 Rubik's Cube scramble and are given 1 hour to write down the shortest possible solution to that scramble. It has some of the lowest participation out of all cubing events, but I personally love the event! It is fun to try to work out creative solutions to the puzzles that don't rely on fast finger movements and repetition of a method. Infact, many of the main 3x3 solving methods aren't great for FMC. Instead, FMC relies heavily on blockbuilding and EO with no specific method in mind. It also employs a number of tricks that can only be done in FMC, like Normal-Inverse-Scramble-Switch (NISS) and insertions.

If you want to get into FMC, I highly recommend the bible of FMC, Sebastiano Tronto's FMC Tutorial. As of the time of this writing (June 2021), he holds the world record for FMC single at just 16 moves, which he will likely hold for a very long time, considering only 1 in 442 scrambles can be solved in 15 moves or less.
