Weather Links

As a weather enthusiast, I've always found it difficult to find resources on the internet to find meteorological data, so I've compiled a set of links that I believe will be helpful resources for weather enthusiasts such as myself. There are a few charts for different categories ranked roughly by how usefel I find them.

Tropical Weather

LinkOrganizationDescription
https://nhc.noaa.govNHC @ NWSOfficial National Hurricane Center guidance on tropical systems. 2 Day & 5 Day tropical weather outlooks and impact guidance.
https://www.tropicaltidbits.comDr. Levi CowanCollection of commonly used tools. Blog posts with a professional opinion on US-affecting tropical cyclones, current storm information, satellite imagery, forecast models, aircraft reconnaissance data, climate analysis tools.
http://tropic.ssec.wisc.edu/SSEC @ UW-MadisonWide variety of specific products. ADT, AMSU, ARCHER, SATCON for estimating TC strength and position, MIMIC microwave imagery, basin-wide wind analysis, SAL tracking.
https://rammb-data.cira.colostate.edu/tc_realtime/CIRA @ CSUCompilation and archive of storm imagery and data. Many graphs of numerical product data, mainly regarding TC intensity forecasts.
https://www.ospo.noaa.gov/Products/ocean/sst/contour/index.htmlOSPO @ NOAADetailed Sea Surface Temperature analysis images.
http://www.atmos.albany.edu/student/ppapin/Phillipe PapinHyperspecific TC research. Authored paper on Central American Gyres, website has great CAG Diagnostics charts. Historical IR imagery.
http://moe.met.fsu.edu/~rhart/web.phpRobert Hart @ FSUVarious TC research products.
http://spaghettimodels.com/Busy webpage compiling graphical products from many sources.
https://www.weathernerds.org/tc_guidance/WeatherNerdsSpaghetti plots.
https://web.uwm.edu/hurricane-models/models/index.htmlUW
http://www.marineheatwaves.org/tracker.htmlMarine heatwaves (I have not been able to load this website).
http://www.cyclocane.com/
